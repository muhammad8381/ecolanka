About Our Resources

  1. Rubber

    Sri Lanka is known as the leader of the global natural rubber industry for manufacturing high-quality natural rubber and rubber-based products. Initiated in 1876 by the planting of 1,919 seedlings, the rubber industry in Sri Lanka gave birth to an excellent and successful supply chain. Because of their durability and superior quality, products manufactured in Sri Lanka are globally recognised. Our partner company Waves Lanka uses Sri Lankan rubber for the manufacturing of their flip flops.

  2. Bamboo

    Bamboos occur naturally in Sri Lanka in all three major climatic areas. In the high altitudinal montane region of the central hill country, seven of the ten endemic species are present, two are strictly restricted in the dry zone, and one occurs in wetlands, cleared rain forests and forest gaps.   3. Elephant Waste

    The Elephant dung and other waste matter from Elephants are sourced from local Elephants in Lanka by Eco Maximus. Variations in the diet, age and dental status of the elephant will give a unique colour and texture to each batch of paper. The colour varies depending on the form of food eaten: coconut, kitul or jak. The texture depends on whether or not the elephant can chew the food. Although half-digested fiber makes the paper coarser, fully digested fiber gives the paper a smooth finish..The end product is chlorine and acid free and is manufactured in an ecological manner
